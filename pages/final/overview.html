Final Project, by Jake Zhong

For my final data visualization project, I decided to make a series of data visualization implementations to analyze the real estate market of the bay area, for example, a comparison of the average value of houses from different cities, the trend of the value of houses from 2015 to 2020, etc.

Overall Narrative

About Dataset

More specifically, I’m using the data type of ZHVI All Homes (SFR, Condo/Co-op) Time Series ($) and Monthly Home Sales (Number, Seasonally Adjusted) by filtering them by the geography of cities.

Because I only want to do my data visualization based on the bay area, I also removed those cities outside the bay area and only kept the list of cities in the bay area.

About Goals

My story starts from a “common sense” phenomenon, the houses in the bay area are almost the most expensive ones around the United States. It’s highly likely because of the large number of big companies, especially IT companies that provide a lot of job opportunities. These companies attract many employees to come, work, and stay for a while and we know a house is demanding for whoever wants to stay wherever unless they want to be “homeless”.

However, from an investment perspective, some questions come to our mind in the first place, how much does it really cost to buy a house in the bay area? When and where is the best option to for us to make a deal? I think these questions are worth noting for those who are interested in investing in real estate in the bay area, especially those who want to stay in bay area for the long term.

So, I want to create some data visualizations to demonstrate the average house price of different cities and count of house sales and the trend of the average house price and count of house sales in the past 5 years (2015 - 2019) and hope that it can answer my questions.

About Location

The data of the whole United States is too big, so I want to focus only on the bay area, considering that it has one of the most valuable real estate markets and audiences. Besides that, it’s more related to myself, since I personally live in the bay area and will likely keep living here for a while in the future. So, knowing this real estate data can also benefit me if I consider buying a house in this area.

Why is the Trend?

When an investor wants to invest a product, I think the most important questions to ask are is it a good price already and how many people will buy it. So I list the average price of the house price from different cities in the past 5 years and the total count of house sales in 2019 in order to make a trend and see if it raised or dropped so people know what might be the upcoming outcome.

Why a Map?

A map is also helpful because it’s easier for people to see the actual location of the data and find out the cluster of which cities have the similar data.

Planed Visualizations

Ideally, I want to create 3 separate data visualization implementations including a bar chart, line chart and a map.

Bar chart

I will list different cities in the bay area and calculate their average house price and make them into a bar chart for comparison. For this visualization, I will focus on the data from 2019 - 2020, and break them down by quarters. The reason is that I think today’s price is the most important factor when we consider buying something, and the past market may not make sense to today much.

Line chart

For this chart, I will use data between 2015 - 2020. Although 2020’s data is not complete, it still plays an important role in our decision making because there are just so many things happening already this year and we don’t know if they have an impact on the market at this point, so let’s find out. My idea is to use 4 quarters a year to separate them in order to check that house price changes season by season.

Map

I am not one hundred sure about what exactly I want to do with a map since the dataset doesn’t have geographic information, such as the log and lat. But now I am thinking to count the number of houses sold and the price range of each city. Differing from the bar chart, the map is not just focusing on the price, but also the number of the sold houses, because investors may not only want to know who is more expensive, but also who sell faster and more.
